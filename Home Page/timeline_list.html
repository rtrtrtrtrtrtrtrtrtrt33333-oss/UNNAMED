Beginning of all

Architects

The first and oldest era. An age shaped by the Architects, whose works would outlive their creators by countless generations. Their cities touched the skies, their machines endured for millennia, and their disappearance marked the end of one world and the beginning of another.

The golden age

The Renaissancce

The golden age of artificial civilization. Freed from the shadow of the Architects, nations flourished, discoveries reshaped the known world, and great empires rose from the ruins of the old age. For many, it remains the brightest chapter in recorded history.

The Long War, beginning

What began as rivalry soon became conquest. Borders shifted, alliances fractured, and the ambitions of young empires ignited a conflict that would outlast those who started it. Few understood that the age of peace had already ended.

Act II

The Long War, the bloodletting

The war reached its height. Entire worlds were consumed by endless campaigns, countless cities vanished beneath fire and steel, and rivers of synthetic blood flowed through battlefields that no longer served any purpose beyond war itself.

Act III

The Long War, pre-ascension

As centuries became millennia, faith succeeded where victory had failed. New doctrines spread across exhausted civilizations, promising salvation beyond conflict. Among them grew a belief that would change the fate of the world forever.

The Ascension Era

The age of restoration. Ancient dreams became reality as organic life returned to a world that had long forgotten it. Celebrated as a miracle by some and a triumph by others, it marked the beginning of humanity's oldest fantasy and civilization's final mistake.
